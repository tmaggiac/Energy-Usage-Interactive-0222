This dek will talk about the relationship between the forest elephants and the fruit trees within the Gabonese forests. Forest elephants like to eat fruit, which in turn are very reliant on the elephants for reproduction. Omphalocarpum fruit which are 8 inches in diameter, have extremely thick skin, and large skinny seeds. This fruit can only be eaten by elephants. Climate change is deminishing the fruits so elephants are becoming hungry, and thus raiding crops.
Source: World Bank (2015)
